Entry class

Cooper Class

Eligible cars are the BMW R50 & R53 Mini Cooper / Cooper S, produced 2001–2006.

How the class works

Cooper Class cars remain largely OEM, with a defined list of permitted modifications. Parity comes from control components — a Link G4X control ECU, the RubberCraft SP-01R control tyre, a 1200 kg minimum weight with driver, and commercially available 98 RON pump fuel only.

Engine internals are free but must not exceed an 8.5:1 compression ratio, and the factory supercharger must remain unmodified. Any modification judged to give a performance advantage moves the car to the Unlimited Class.

Unlimited Class

Built to Motorsport Australia Group 3D Sport Sedan regulations, subject to SMC amendments.

How the class works

Engine internals and gearbox are free, and no minimum weight is enforced. Cars must remain front-wheel-drive floorpan cars as defined in the Group 3D regulations, with the engine block sourced from a BMC or BMW Mini and mounted no further back than the firewall.

Maximum rim size is 17 x 9 inches, overall track width is limited to 1875 mm, ABS is free, and all cars run the RubberCraft RC001 control tyre in 220/620-R17 or 240/610-R17.
